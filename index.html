Problem-solving Learnings after Midterms Fall 2025

Oct 24th, 2025

12:51 right now. Waiting to head onto the GO Bus back to Square one in QNC right now. Finished MTE 252 exam at 10:30 then rowed an 8:04 2k.

This is What I learned from the exams. 1) I feel that I write really fast, so I am able to do the naive approach, which works, but is objectively slower, although it is more active. I learned that my fear of being slow and stagnant is greater than my fear of not solving the problem in the most optimal manner. I really want to go fast. But I learned that it's not about doing a method that works the quickest (this I can do in my sleep), but it's about finding the fastest method first, and doing that fast. 2 methods, same solution, the faster one is generally better. But sometimes doing the slower method is more fortuitous. Anyways, more methods is always better. So, I learned that just becaues I can do a problem in a gaurantted way, and just write very fast, doesn't mean its actually the fastest although it feels fast, because, i'm writing pen to paper. 2) Studying is a "questioning battle". Overstudying is a real problem. But, it is my belief that the result you get on an exam is the same result you would've gotten without studying. Studying just reduces cognitive load, which isn't a problem during your primal exam state, where you have the will to derive equatoins and cognitive load isn't a problem. So I learned that studying is really just questioning oneself. Overstudying yields to mindlessness, like in point 1. Where you just do any method, One needs to be calm and overstudying generates anxiety and dumb methods (that do work) though. 3) I learned that I don't need to study so much, I'll still work super hard, but on questioning myself and my knowledge, rather than being in papers and lessons. I'll work on my social skills and become smarter in choosing the fastest method, rather doing a method in the fastest way. I'll study by being productive and study like solving a problem rather than trying to find problems.

Being With Others

Aug 26th, 2025

This blog would actually be me catching up on my thinkings on Sunday August 17th 2025, the day I realized I was all alone in my Room. I was on coop in Spring 2025, and my friends on study term, so when their exams ended on August 15th and they had all moved out on August 17th, I realized why it is so important to be with others. Being with others makes you more conscious of your own actions/behaviours. And being conscious of what you do is important in being self-aware and becoming a better person. This applies both in every aspect. If you want to become better at anything, do it with others. Doing it with others makes you more conscious of your mistakes, or what you are doing. Not to say, you're just held more accountable in situation dealing with others, but one simply unconsciously thinks about others when being with others and this is highly beneficial. When you realize that everyone is going through life and that whatever bad has happened, other peoples' time other has gone by, and it is just a passing of time almost. What I'm trying to say is that struggles are more seen as part of life when you live with others, as you subconsciously see other peoples' journey and know that it's all a journey. Additionally, simple things become conscious when living with others. A key fact is that you become more conscious of what you do. Not in feeling self-conscious/shy, but you realize what is right for you to do because you know what other people should/ shouldn't be doing. For example, if you should be reading, but you just came back from work and want to watch Youtube videos, seeing other people, makes you, one, externalize your inner desires, and you see that it's just your conscience, and two, seeing others makes you realize that I should probably just read. I think that when one sees someone, you subconsciously see yourself in that person in a way. And you see yourself in a way such that you subconsciously apply what they/you should be doing, such that you then do the right thing.
I know firsthand how easy it is to waste entire days without energy; not knowing why you just can't get to work or do anything productive. "Why can't I help myself?" "Why am I just ruining myself, I'm not getting any joy from wasting my time, but I can't do anything else". Yes, some things are difficult to achieve. But I believe this was because I was living alone and had no inner motivation and perspective on life. Since I was all alone, I had no accountability to myself or others. I had no conscience telling me what I should be or should not be doing. I had no inspiration in other people to aid in my life. I was super motivated to do work, but couldn't do it because no one was with me. And it just felt so suffocating.

Being with others makes you conscious and aware of what you do, because we see ourselves in others. Also we do a lot of things for others and to better our lives to better other peoples' lives. So be with good people you care about, and that care about you!

Learning is Not the Same as Working

July 20, 2024

Something I came to realize is that learning is not the same as working. When you're working on a project, there are two parts, the learning and the working. The learning is the understanding of what to do, why you do it this way, and what the results mean. Working is the setting of everything up, scheduling the workflow, and thinking about how the steps interact. Working uses current knowledge and is like solving a puzzle. Something I realized is that while working is quantized by getting something done, learning is not. So people generally try to work more efficiently to get something done or configured. And you CAN do that, that's good, since once you figure something out, its done. But for learning, you should actually try to take it slow and be inefficient. You should try to explore things and form new conclusions. So what I learned is that rushing work is OK, but rushing learning is very bad. Try to be inefficient, and read and understand every word. Form connections from what you previously know. A project is not a bunch of work that needs get done and the pursuit of the final object. There is a lot of learning, and that learning needs to be done well and slowly and methodically. This learning is the most important thing of a project that makes sure the project comes to completion, not the work, if you understand what I mean.
